Hi, my name is Jacob Marshall. I am a researcher, a software engineer, and an undergraduate student at the University of Kansas.
I am currently mentored and employed by Dr. Drew Davidson as part of the Padlock Research Laboratory at KU and am conducting research on drone sensor input-spoofing attacks. When I’m not studying or doing research, I have a variety of other personal projects that keep me busy.

Above all, I like working on hard problems. The ones that haven’t been solved before, and for good reason. I consider my interests within the realm of computer science to be pretty broad. I enjoy learning about the applications of software to financial markets. I’m curious about how we can continue to ensure that systems are made secure from attacks by ever-adapting adversaries. I want to find out how humans can better interact harmoniously with robotic systems.

I am currently in the late stages of the application process for several PhD programs, but have also been on the lookout for top-of-the-line full-time positions in industry. I hope to have my future sorted out by the end of April.

When I'm not in front of a computer screen, I immensely enjoy spending time outdoors, doing anything from soccer, to mountain biking, to hiking. I also enjoy playing board games and card games, and hope to become a better cook someday.
