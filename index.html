Hi, my name is Jacob Marshall. I am a researcher, a software engineer, and a PhD student at the University of Kansas. I am currently mentored and employed by Dr. Drew Davidson as part of the Padlock Research Laboratory at KU, where I am conducting research on drone sensor input-spoofing attacks. I enjoy learning about the applications of software to financial markets. I’m curious about how we can continue to ensure that systems are made secure from attacks by ever-adapting adversaries. I want to discover how AI can continue to push the boundaries of modern computation.
