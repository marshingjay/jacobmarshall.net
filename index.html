About me

Hi, my name is Jacob Marshall. I am a researcher, a software engineer, and an undergraduate student at the University of Kansas. I am currently mentored and employed by Dr. Drew Davidson as part of the Padlock Research Laboratory and am conducting research on drone sensor input spoofing attacks. I will be graduating from the University of Kansas in May 2020 and will commence my postgraduate studies in the fall in order to pursue a PhD in Computer Science. I don't like to box myself in to just one area of Computer Science, as I find most of the problems within Computer Science interesting. However, if I had to pick my favorite areas those would currently be Security, HCI, and Robotics. My long-term goals aren't set in stone yet, I find research fascinating but could also see myself moving to the start-up sphere after finishing graduate school if a good oppurtunity preseneted itself. When I'm not in front of a computer screen, the things I enjoy include running, hiking, playing Magic: the Gathering, and spending time with family and friends.
